Common Questions

How long does a project usually take?
Small projects (e.g., one illustration) typically take about one week. Bigger projects usually take 2–4 weeks, depending on scope.
How many concepts do you provide?
At the start, we align on the idea and I deliver two fully developed concept sketches. Each concept comes with its own story, meaning, and visual logic.
How many revision rounds are included?
We lock the direction at the sketch/concept stage, then I refine it with your feedback while keeping the overall vision consistent. Small tweaks and clarifications are included (up to ~10), but the final artistic direction stays with me.
Do you deliver source files?
Yes — I provide the files you need for your use case, including print-ready and high-resolution digital versions. Source files can be included when required and agreed in advance.
What does your process look like, step by step?
We start with a call to explore the idea, goals, and best execution options, then sign a contract. If it’s a physical space, I visit (or work from materials), build a plan, share the initial vision, and then deliver final files.
How does pricing and payment work (deposit, milestones)?
I work with a 50% deposit for projects of any size. Payment terms are clearly stated in the contract we sign before starting.
Do you take rush projects?
Yes, rush projects are possible depending on my schedule. Timeline and rush fee are discussed individually.
What do you need from me to get started?
A quick conversation about what you have and what you want to achieve, plus access to your materials or space (if applicable). After that, I take the lead and handle the process.
Can we work together if my business is in another country?
Absolutely — I work internationally and we can run everything via calls, messages, and mockups. For physical applications, I prepare clear production-ready files and guides tailored to your format.
Can we plan a long-term collaboration?
Yes — long-term projects are welcome once we align on the scope, timeline, and working rhythm. I can lead the visual direction as an art director throughout the collaboration.
